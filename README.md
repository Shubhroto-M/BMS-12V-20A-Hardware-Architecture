12V 20A High-Current Battery Management System (BMS)Role: Hardware & Thermal ArchitectureStatus: V0.2 Blueprint Complete (Validated via Ansys Simulation)The ObjectiveArchitected and routed a custom 12V, 20A continuous-draw Battery Management System designed for high-current robotics and electric power applications. The design prioritizes extreme hardware reliability, completely bypassing firmware-dependent microcontrollers in favor of hardware-level fault protection.Core ArchitectureLogic Controller: Texas Instruments BQ77915. Chosen for its standalone, firmware-less hardware protection (Overvoltage, Undervoltage, Overcurrent, and Short-Circuit).Power Gating: Implemented a back-to-back (Source-to-Source) MOSFET topology on the low side. This creates a true bidirectional block, ensuring that charging and discharging paths are completely isolated when a fault state is triggered, preventing body-diode bypass.Transient Protection: Integrated TVS (Transient Voltage Suppression) diodes to armor the board against inductive voltage spikes from motors and reverse-polarity events.The Engineering Challenge: Thermal BottleneckingDuring the initial validation of V0.1, I ran a localized thermal stress test using Ansys Discovery. The simulation revealed a critical failure point under maximum load:Pushing 20 Amps continuously through standard 9.3mΩ SMD MOSFETs generated nearly 4 Watts of localized Joule heating ($I^2R$).Due to the low thermal conductivity of the standard FR4 PCB substrate ($0.3 \text{ W/m·K}$), the heat was unable to dissipate efficiently. The simulation showed theoretical junction temperatures exceeding 595°C—a catastrophic failure state.The V0.2 Pivot (Solving the Thermal Crisis)Rather than increasing the PCB footprint to accommodate bulky, expensive aluminum heatsinks, I re-engineered the power path at the silicon level.Component Swap: Replaced the generic silicon with premium Infineon BSC010N04LS MOSFETs featuring an ultra-low $R_{DS(on)}$ of 1.0mΩ.The Result: This pivot reduced localized heat generation by nearly 90% (dropping from ~4W down to 0.4W per chip).Final Validation: Rerunning the Ansys simulation with a targeted 0.5mm local fidelity mesh confirmed the fix. The concentrated heat was entirely eliminated, allowing the custom copper pours to passively dissipate the remaining thermal load into the ambient air, peaking at a completely safe 60°C under maximum continuous draw.Industrial Design & EnclosureTo complete the hardware package, I designed a custom, manufacturable 3D enclosure in Fusion 360. The casing features precision IO cutouts for the high-current screw terminals and JST balancing leads, ensuring a flush fit and mechanical strain relief for the PCB.
